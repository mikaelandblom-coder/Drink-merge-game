🎵 Sound Lab

Audition synth sounds here before wiring them into audio.js. On deck: the Farm — Sprout Valley garden sound profile and the Paris café profile, plus a shared game-over sound. The Melody Lane material that already shipped is kept at the bottom for reference.

🥐 Paris — Le Petit Café · merge pop candidates

A · Porcelain & silver spoon — ✅ WINNER, shipped as popCafe

Dainty china "ting" — a teaspoon tapped on a teacup, with a faint upward curl. Brighter and lighter than Kyoto's ceramic profile; pitch rises with tier.

B · Pâtisserie poof

Soft cream-puff squish with a powdered-sugar sparkle on top. The squishiest option — the items are pastries, so the merge sounds like pastry.

C · Musette accordion

A short wet-tuned accordion note per merge — instant Paris. Tiers climb an F-major scale, so a combo cascade plays a little melody (same trick as Melody Lane).

☕ Collision & coins (Paris)

Collision: saucer tick (delicate china tap) vs pastry bump (soft dusted thud). Coins: the shipping default blip vs a little service bell "ding" with a slight pitch wobble per coin so showers don't drill. Coins land ~110ms apart, real game tempo. ✅ WINNERS: pastry bump (clinkCafe) + service bell (coinTickCafe).

🌱 Farm — Sprout Valley · merge pop candidates

A · Sprout bloop

A cute rounded "bloop" that bends upward like something popping out of the soil — the water-droplet / plant-grow sound. Cheeriest, most video-gamey. Pitch climbs a major pentatonic so a cascade stays sweet.

B · Woody marimba

Round triangle tone + marimba's 4th-harmonic "knock" — warm, woody, cozy. The Stardew-porch option. Tiers climb the scale, so a combo cascade plays a little melody.

C · Ocarina bloom

A short breathy pennywhistle / ocarina note with a soft attack — folk and pastoral, the most "melodic instrument" of the three. Cascades sound like a little tune.

🌿 Collision & coins (Farm)

Collision: soft dirt thud (earthy, crumbly — produce settling in the bed) vs a wooden knock (hitting the raised-bed frame). Coins: wooden xylophone tick vs a bright sparkle chime. Coins land ~110ms apart, real game tempo.

🔚 Game over — shared by all maps

Game-over sound

Today NOTHING plays when a run ends — the only game-over-screen sound is the new-best fanfare(). The winner gets called from showGameOver() in ui.js. Tone target: "the café is closing", not "you failed" — it plays at the end of EVERY run, including great ones. ✅ WINNER: "Gently down", shipped as gameOver() — called from showGameOver() on every run EXCEPT a new best, where the fanfare plays instead (they'd clash together).

🎸 Melody Lane — shipped reference

A · Music box / celesta

Sine fundamental + bright inharmonic bell partials. Toy-like, twinkly, clearly "musical."

B · Plucked string (ukulele / harp)

Sawtooth through a decaying lowpass with a tiny noise pluck. Warm, organic, folk-instrument feel.

C · Marimba / vibraphone

Triangle fundamental + strong 4th harmonic (marimba's signature). Round, woody, mellow.

🪙 Coin & collision (Melody Lane)

The coin shower plays an ascending pentatonic run (one octave above the merges) instead of a repeated blip — a small merge is a short rise, a big combo payout is a long harp cascade. Collisions get a soft hollow-body knock instead of a metallic tink. Coins land ~110ms apart, matching the real game tempo.

🏆 Personal best (high-score flourish)

Plays on the game-over screen when a run beats that variant's best (the game's fanfare(), map-agnostic). The brass fanfare won and ships today; the others are kept as also-rans. "Shipped" mirrors audio.js exactly — use it (or the checkbox in the game-over card above) to check the new game-over sound against it.

Tip: hit "Play cascade" on each card to compare how a 9-chain combo sounds — that's the sound you'll hear most in a good run. Then try a couple of single tiers to judge the timbre up close.
